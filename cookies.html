Information about how we use cookies on our website

AquaNestle Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 1, 2023

This Cookie Policy explains how AquaNestle Irrigation Services ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies when you visit our website. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy. You can manage your cookie preferences at any time through your browser settings or the cookie consent manager on our website.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the website owners. Cookies typically contain anonymous information such as a unique identifier, the site name, and some digits and numbers. They cannot carry viruses or install malware on your device. We use both session cookies (which expire when you close your browser) and persistent cookies (which stay on your device until deleted). Cookies help us understand how our site is being used and improve your browsing experience.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use several types of cookies: Strictly necessary cookies (essential for website functionality), performance cookies (help us improve the site), functionality cookies (remember your preferences), and targeting cookies (used by advertising networks). Each type serves a different purpose: essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are required for the site to function properly, analytics cookies help us understand visitor behavior, preference cookies remember your settings, and marketing cookies help deliver relevant ads. We may also use local storage and tracking pixels as part of our cookie technology.

3. Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for you to navigate our website and use its features, such as accessing secure areas or making purchases. Without these cookies, services you've asked for like shopping baskets or secure logins cannot be provided. They include cookies that remember your actions as you navigate from page to page, helping avoid the need to reauthenticate or reprocess each action. These cookies don't gather information about you that could be used for marketing purposes and don't remember your preferences beyond your current visit. They are usually set in response to actions you take such as logging in or filling in forms.

4. Performance Cookies

Performance cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, like which pages are visited most often and if users experience error messages. These cookies don't collect information that identifies a visitor - all information is aggregated and therefore anonymous. We use Google Analytics cookies to help us improve our site by understanding what content is most valuable to our visitors. The data collected includes the number of visitors, where visitors come from, and the pages they visit. This helps us ensure the site is meeting user needs and identify areas for enhancement.

5. Functionality Cookies

Functionality cookies allow our website to remember choices you make and provide enhanced, more personal features. These cookies can be used to remember your login details, language preference, region selection, or font size settings. They may also be used to provide services you've requested such as watching a video or commenting on a blog. These cookies cannot track your browsing activity on other websites. The information collected may be anonymized and cannot track your browsing activity on other websites. Some videos on our website may use cookies to anonymously gather statistics on how you got there and what videos you visited.

6. Targeting/Advertising Cookies

These cookies are used to deliver ads more relevant to you and your interests based on your browsing history. They are also used to limit the number of times you see an ad and help measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns. They are typically placed by advertising networks with our permission. The cookies remember that you've visited a website and this information is shared with other organizations such as advertisers. If you don't allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising. Note that opting out doesn't mean you won't see ads - just that they'll be less relevant.

7. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by services that appear on our website but are not under our control. These include cookies from social media platforms (if you share content on social media), embedded videos (like YouTube), and analytics services. Third-party cookies are subject to their respective privacy policies. We don't control how these cookies are used, so we suggest you check their websites for more information about their cookies and how to manage them. We use reasonable efforts to implement only reputable third-party services that respect user privacy, but cannot be responsible for their cookie practices.

8. How Long Do Cookies Stay On My Device?

The lifespan of a cookie depends on its purpose: Session cookies are temporary and expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain until they expire or you delete them from your browser cache. First-party cookies we set typically last from a few hours up to 2 years. Third-party cookies may have different durations set by their providers. You can manually delete stored cookies through your browser settings at any time. Some cookies refresh each time you visit our website, while others have fixed expiration dates. The expiration period is set when the cookie is created and may vary across different cookie categories.

9. Managing and Disabling Cookies

You can control and/or delete cookies as you wish through your browser settings. You can delete all cookies that are already on your computer and set most browsers to prevent them from being placed. However, if you do this, you may need to manually adjust some preferences each time you visit our site, and some functionality may not work. Browser settings typically allow you to see what cookies you have and delete them individually or block cookies from particular sites. Each browser is different - check your browser's "Help" menu to learn how to change cookie preferences.

10. Cookie Consent Manager

Our cookie consent manager tool allows you to selectively choose which categories of cookies you accept. You can access the manager via the button in our cookie notice banner or through a link in the website footer. The tool provides clear information about each cookie category and its purpose. Your preferences are stored in a cookie so we can remember your choices on subsequent visits. If you clear your browser cookies, you'll need to reset your preferences. The tool gives you granular control over non-essential cookies while ensuring essential functionality is maintained.

11. Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies completely, our website may not function properly. Essential features like account logins, shopping carts, and secure transactions may fail. Disabling performance cookies means we can't gather data to improve our site. Rejecting functionality cookies means the site won't remember your preferences. Blocking advertising cookies won't stop ads - they'll just become less relevant. Some embedded content (like videos) might not work without third-party cookies. We recommend keeping strictly necessary cookies enabled for full functionality and selectively disabling others based on your comfort level.

12. Cookies We Use

Below is a detailed list of all cookies we use on our website with their purpose, category, and duration:

Essential: session_id (end of session), csrf_token (1 year)

13. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that lets you tell websites you don't want to have your online activities tracked. Currently, we do not respond to browser "Do Not Track" signals as there's no industry standard for implementation. However, we provide you the tools to control tracking through our cookie consent manager. Even if we receive a Do Not Track signal, essential cookies required for site operation will still be used. We monitor industry developments regarding Do Not Track standards and will revisit this policy if standards emerge.
