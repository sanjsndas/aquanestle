Expert Irrigation Tips

Watering in the Early Morning

The best time to water your plants is early in the morning, between 5 AM and 9 AM. This allows the water to reach the roots before the heat of the day causes evaporation. Morning watering also helps prevent fungal diseases that can occur when plants remain damp overnight.

Deep Watering vs. Frequent Light Watering

Most plants benefit from deep, infrequent watering rather than frequent light sprinklings. Deep watering encourages roots to grow downward, creating stronger, more drought-resistant plants. A general rule is to water until the soil is moist 6-12 inches deep, depending on the plant type.

Mulching for Moisture Retention

A 2-3 inch layer of organic mulch (like wood chips or straw) around your plants can reduce water evaporation from the soil by up to 70%. Mulch also regulates soil temperature and suppresses weeds that compete with your plants for water.
